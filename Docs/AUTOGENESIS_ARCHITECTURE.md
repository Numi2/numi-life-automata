# Numi Automata Metal Architecture

## Product Contract

Numi Automata opens at spinor scale with a persistent `1024 x 1024` two-component complex field, one `193 x 193` chemical and geological substrate, zero physical components, and zero cells. Spinor coherence must first produce catalyst, stored energy, and a closed membrane. A locally self-maintaining membrane can atomically claim the first component handle, claim one generation-tagged heritable-program slot, and initialize exactly one cell. A pool of 9,216 generation-tagged component handles shares one global pool of 9,216 persistent cells and a recyclable pool of 4,096 live inherited programs. Cell storage position does not determine component membership, and component ownership does not determine inherited-program identity. Additional component and cell records are claimed only by membrane disconnection, cell division, or an explicit **Add colony** action. Camera movement, observer inference, and rendering never create or delete state.

The UI exposes observation rather than simulation configuration: pause, restart, add a founder, choose speed or diagnostic mode, navigate physical scale, and follow or browse organisms. Population, hunters, resources, turnover, events, and quantum norm are measured from live state.

## Physical Components

Each `AgentState` stores:

- Generation-tagged storage identity and occupancy.
- World-space position and velocity.
- Three inherited four-component gene vectors.
- Last measured pursuit, attack-contact, and threat behavior.
- Mean cell energy, occupied-cell biomass proxy, numerical age, and component-descent depth.
- The dominant inherited-program index and program-replication generation used for summaries.

`nucleateAutogenicFounder` accepts only a local maximum with enough biomass, stored energy, catalyst, and membrane. Its inherited phase and metabolic biases come from the local spinor and chemistry rather than a predefined founder genome. `evolveAgents` integrates only force, torque, mass, and moment of inertia reduced from cells during the previous update. GPU union-find allocates an independent component handle to every nonempty membrane-connected root after physical separation. No ATP, integrity, cell-count, detachment, age, observer, or dwell-time threshold decides whether that root receives identity. A temporary handle-allocation miss defers reassignment without deleting its cells.

There is no separate organism body shader. Ecology, organism, and cellular views submit the same GPU-compacted living cells and construct a tissue contour from their simulated membrane vertices. Predatory protrusions, armor, sensors, and locomotor extensions become visible only where exposed cells physically deform their membranes under the corresponding inherited regulatory output. Lineage color and physiological overlays describe those cells; they do not instantiate anatomy or assign a species.

## Persistent Cells

`CellState` is a 288-byte record containing organism-relative position and velocity plus seventeen state vectors:

- Physiology: ATP, biomass, cell-cycle phase, membrane integrity.
- Phenotype: adhesion, contractility, resource-A uptake, resource-B uptake.
- Signals: central morphogen, peripheral morphogen, stress, apoptosis activation.
- Interaction: nearest-contact direction, contact-cycle brake, direct mechanics-to-voltage contribution.
- Dynamics: membrane voltage, excitable recovery, oscillator phase, intrinsic frequency.
- Mechanics: contractile activation, extracellular strain, wave speed, local phase coherence.
- Energetics: harvest, maintenance, active work, dissipation.
- Regulation: proliferation, adhesive-core fate, contractile-edge fate, repair.
- Regulation B: permeability, secretion, apoptosis suppression, motility.
- Resonance: resonator displacement, velocity, response amplitude, previous strain input.
- Membrane: polygon area, perimeter, shape index, transmitted junction force.
- Signaling: dimensionless calcium-like activity, ERK-like activity, refractory state, neighbor signal input.
- Signal causality: mechanics-to-Ca* edge effect, Ca*-to-ERK* edge effect, ERK*-traction magnitude, signaling ATP cost.
- Tissue geometry: vertex-derived outward normal, exposed membrane fraction, physical detachment score.
- Tissue force: local traction/contact force, contact damage, signed trophic transfer.
- Environment: substrate forcing, barrier load, environmental forcing frequency, frequency match.
- Development: persistent tissue polarity, continuous fate memory, junction morphogen transport.

Each cell has a hot 32-byte identity record containing its current physical component owner, inherited-program index and slot generation, monotonic permanent cell ID, and current union-find root. Parent-cell ID remains persistent in a separate 4-byte cold genealogy buffer written only at founder creation and division. A separate 16-byte cold interaction vector stores signed ATP transfer, incompatible-program rejection, reciprocal recognition compatibility, and local net energetic contribution without expanding the 288-byte hot cell record. All 9,216 cells share one atomic allocation pool. Per-owner and per-root segmented linked lists are rebuilt on the GPU, so tissues need not occupy contiguous records and component-specific work does not rescan the complete cell pool. `evolveOrganismCells` validates the generation-tagged program reference, then reads developmental topology, mechanochemical gains, morphogen kinetics, resonant tuning, recognition coordinates, and social-control coefficients. It computes program-specific ATP sharing and rejection, voltage and phase coupling, junction-mediated morphogen transport, resonant strain sensing, contact-propagated mechanochemical signaling, exposure-gated substrate claims, ATP accounting, ATP-dependent quiescence, conserved biomass catabolism, sparse graph dynamics, phenotype differentiation, cycle progression, and hunger-weighted traction. The component origin remains a coordinate frame and supplies no cell force, boundary direction, detachment factor, or hard radius. The membrane ATP setpoint and permeability turnover fall continuously with metabolic readiness, while complete ATP loss remains terminal. `evolveCellMembranes` advances twelve explicit vertices per occupied cell. A collision-free `128 x 128` GPU spatial grid resolves same-owner and cross-owner membrane support, persistent junctions, equal-and-opposite pair impulses, localized damage, breach-gated trophic transfer, and local connectivity candidates. `measureCellMembraneExposure` tests every physical edge against nearby same-owner polygons directly in their shared tissue coordinates. `divideAndReduceOrganismCells` checks every pre-existing cell's local division state, claims global free cells without selecting a component-wide winner, and reduces only measured exposed segments into covariance, axes, extents, polarity, force, torque, contact, trophic state, inherited-program composition, recognition, rejection, exchange, contribution, environmental forcing, and developmental observables.

Each 128-byte inherited-program payload stores three trait vectors, two ligand coordinates, two receptor coordinates, fusion investment, ATP-sharing gain, incompatible-cell rejection gain, propagule-transmission gain, and immutable provenance for up to two generation-tagged program parents. Its independent 16-byte slot state stores occupancy, living-cell reference count, slot generation, and lineage hash. Every program read validates `(index, generation)`. Division retains the reference; cell death releases it; owner reassignment retains the descendant before releasing the source. A zero reference count atomically returns the payload slot to the free pool. Permanent ancestry remains in the immutable 80-byte lineage-event ring rather than pinning dead program payloads.

A live program references a 160-byte developmental header, sixteen 32-byte node slots, forty-eight 32-byte directed-edge slots, and one 32-byte resonance record. A node stores bias, response rate, sensor coupling, actuator coupling, sensor index, actuator mask, innovation ID, and activity. Generic positive state feedback gives each active node hysteresis, allowing the inherited graph to settle into persistent attractors without a cell-type table. An edge stores weight, strain-dependent plasticity, endpoints, innovation ID, and activity. Sixteen inputs carry ATP, voltage, strain, contact density, receptor-weighted intracellular and extracellular morphogen state, local opportunity minus stress, resonant response, membrane damage and deformation, shape, junction strain plus wound cue, toxin/waste/wound state, resource contrast, program conflict, junction conductance, membrane exposure, and developmental polarity plus fate memory. Eight output channels control proliferation, adhesion, contraction, repair, permeability, secretion, apoptosis suppression, and motility. The header additionally stores heritable mechanochemical, morphogen, junction-material, and ecological-response coefficients. Founders start with twelve active nodes and twenty active edges; topology remains bounded at sixteen nodes and forty-eight edges. Recognition and social variables are dimensionless model controls, not molecular identities or calibrated immune kinetics.

Each persistent same-owner junction is a 64-byte material record. Cell-local regulation and the two programs' inherited coefficients set target normal stiffness, viscous damping, transport permeability, and cortical tension. The record also retains remodeling rest length, strain memory, ATP investment, and polarity alignment. Remodeling and transport consume ATP through cellular active work. Detachment, apoptosis, membrane failure, and low ATP therefore alter local connectivity through mechanics; no component-level reproduction instruction severs a tissue.

Morphogens are updated through both the persistent same-owner junction graph and a shared `193 x 193` extracellular developmental field rather than from tissue radius. A junction is eligible only when its permanent-cell fingerprint matches and it was refreshed within two simulation steps. Pair transport is proportional to concentration difference, inherited species-specific diffusivity, junction strength and age, and membrane integrity; measured junction load lowers conductance. Reversing the ordered pair reverses the flux. Exposed cells pay active work to release both ligands into the field. Ligands diffuse and decay independently, receptor-weighted extracellular imbalance enters the same regulatory sensor as intracellular state, and its local gradient biases persistent polarity. A third field channel stores slowly turning-over matrix deposited by adhesive repair programs; matrix improves adhesion-dependent force transmission. A rapidly diffusing wound/remodeling channel raises damage sensors and erodes local matrix. Local activator autocatalysis, inhibitor suppression, inherited production, and inherited first-order decay update intracellular concentrations. No field channel contains a component identifier or target geometry.

Program mutation occurs only during cell division. A daughter normally retains the parent's generation-tagged program reference. A replication error, whose probability depends on inherited fidelity, local ATP, and paid repair, instead claims a recyclable slot and copies then mutates trait vectors, recognition coordinates, social controls, sparse regulatory topology, mechanochemical gains, morphogen kinetics, and resonance. Structural mutation can duplicate or silence nodes and add, remove, or reconnect edges; every new structural record receives a monotonic innovation ID. Mixed-program tissue also permits crossbreeding during division: a distinct compatible cell must be connected to the dividing cell by a recently refreshed mature junction, both programs must invest in transmission, and preparatory exchange consumes active work. Compatibility, donor competence, junction maturity, bilateral investment, and program difference each have explicit minimum gates. Their geometric mean forms a normalized suitability score, which drives a separately capped stochastic crossover probability instead of reusing the raw multi-factor product as a probability. Crossover chooses inherited trait coordinates, regulatory nodes and edges, developmental chemistry, junction material, ecological response, and resonance from both parents; the resulting recyclable program records both exact parent handles. Active repair suppresses crossbreeding preparation. Physical separation only reassigns component coordinates and ownership and never introduces mutation or recombination.

Each program also carries eight inherited resonance parameters: natural frequency, damping ratio, input gain, response threshold, bandwidth, adaptation rate, phase delay, and directional preference. Cells integrate a damped second-order response to extracellular strain rate. Its signed thresholded amplitude enters voltage, Ca* gating, phase, and the regulatory graph. Frequency can adapt only within the inherited bandwidth; reproduction mutates the tuning parameters.

Each reaction tile publishes fixed-point availability for resource A, resource B, and detritus. Cells claim those reservoirs with compare-and-swap; claimed substrate is converted to ATP with an inherited bounded efficiency, and the conversion loss is exported as heat. The tile exchange contains twelve atomic channels: eight settle substrate, detritus, and heat, while four accumulate ATP-paid ligand, matrix, and wound/remodeling output for the next field step. ATP pays maintenance, signaling, contraction, electrical activity, rejection, stress dissipation, secretion, matrix construction/remodeling, genetic-exchange preparation, and a frequency-dependent resonator cost proportional to `(v^2 + omega^2 x^2)(0.00042 + 0.075 f)(0.58 + 0.82 g)`. Integrity loss, stress, wound cue, and contact damage generate cell-local repair urgency. Urgency lowers a cell's ATP potential across junctions, redirects compatible pairwise transfer toward the wound, suppresses discretionary growth and motion, and requests explicit active repair work. Only the paid fraction restores integrity; all of these effects decay continuously as local damage resolves. Maintenance returns 34% to detritus and 66% to heat; biomass loss and death return 72% to detritus and 28% to heat. `reactWorld` settles both exchanges on the following update. A separate ten-channel signed fixed-point audit records substrate input, ATP harvest, ATP-plus-biomass storage change, active work, frequency work, maintenance, heat, detrital energy, internal ATP sharing, and the global conservation residual. No organism-level proxy participates in this ledger.

Ca* and ERK* are dimensionless active-state variables, not concentrations. Contact-weighted neighboring Ca*/ERK* produces junctional propagation. Mechanical resonance, local strain, and transmitted junction force gate Ca* entry; Ca* drives ERK*; ERK* drives evolved motility-dependent traction opposite its local gradient. A refractory state suppresses repeated Ca* entry and propagation. The active states and both edge contributions consume ATP. For every update, the kernel evaluates factual Ca*/ERK* results and a local result with the selected incoming edge set to zero while holding the pre-update state fixed. It retains mechanics-to-Ca* and Ca*-to-ERK* factual-minus-edge-zero differences, ERK*-traction magnitude, and signaling work. These values are model-internal direct terms, not estimates from trajectory covariance.

The reduction kernel emits a 336-byte `CellAggregate`: physiology, morphology, voltage and phase statistics, mechanics, energy terms, actuator vectors, direct equation terms, resonance, polygon geometry, mechanochemical state, exposed-edge covariance axes and extents, polarity, boundary length, net force, torque, contact load, trophic flux, detachment readiness, dominant/non-dominant program fractions, hashed program-richness lower bound, dominant-program index, mean absolute ATP exchange, rejection load, recognition compatibility, net program contribution, environmental forcing, morphogen state, fate memory, junction transport, differentiation, polarity coherence, synthesis, and transport work. Boundary covariance is the analytic line integral over exposed polygon segments, and extents project the segment endpoints rather than cell centers or a synthetic envelope. `evolveAgents` consumes the previous aggregate, while the renderer directly consumes current cells and membrane vertices. This one-step delayed physical feedback avoids a CPU synchronization point while preserving the causal sequence:

```text
substrate chemistry -> cell-local signaling and traction -> membrane geometry and contact -> tissue force and torque -> component motion, cell division, and physical separation
```

Each cell accumulates a signed fixed-point contraction impulse into a `193 x 193 x 2` private atomic buffer. `evolveMechanicalField` consumes and zeros the impulse with relaxed atomic exchange while advancing a damped vector wave over a ping-pong `RGBA16Float` texture pair. Displacement gradients feed cell strain; velocity contributes dissipation; strain changes voltage and reaction chemistry. Cells respond locally through inherited resonance and traction; there is no agent-level vibration steering. This is a closed feedback cycle, not a render-only effect.

The mechanics-to-voltage and mechanics-to-Ca* edges share one intervention gain in `SimulationUniforms`. The normal value is `1`; the ECG control sets it to `0` without stopping mechanics or resonance. The next metric reduction records a single-trajectory response and does not label it a controlled effect. Observational diagnostics use a predeclared one-sample lag between first differences and report Pearson `r`, an effective sample count adjusted by the two series' lag-one autocorrelation, and a Fisher-transformed 95% interval. The effective-count adjustment is an AR(1)-style approximation and does not cover arbitrary long-memory or nonlinear dependence. This remains predictive association rather than causal identification. The **Causal terms** renderer uses cyan for mechanics-to-Ca*, magenta for Ca*-to-ERK*, green for ERK*-traction, and orange for signaling ATP cost. The original mechanics-to-voltage, cycle-drive, contact-suppression, and repair terms remain available in organism state.

Cells produced by division receive a new permanent cell ID and their physical parent's cell ID. ATP and biomass split equally. Morphogens, Ca*/ERK* activity, fate memory, and regulatory-node activity receive bounded opposite partitions that preserve their mean concentration; division does not directly assign actuator outputs or a cell type. The daughter normally retains the same inherited-program index and generation, while mutation or compatible crossbreeding may claim a new program slot. A crossbred division emits the ordinary division record for the physical parent and an additional genetic-donor record for the second program parent. Contact phase coupling depends separately on sender and receiver phenotype, so it is generally nonreciprocal. Mechanical contact is reciprocal: each canonical pair computes actual support vertices once and accumulates equal-and-opposite fixed-point center and vertex impulses. Same-owner adhesive pairs acquire persistent rest distance, strength, age, and load in the junction hash. Unlike-program neighbors compute reciprocal ligand/receptor compatibility. Compatibility gates bilateral ATP sharing; incompatibility and inherited rejection gain impose ATP cost, stress, apoptosis activation, and membrane loss. Atomic union-find labels connected components from membrane support, adhesion, and integrity. Different owners can join only under direct support contact, high reciprocal adhesion and integrity, low stress and predatory investment, reciprocal recognition compatibility, bilateral fusion investment, and a positive multiplicative fusion-drive gate. Eligibility enforces the individual constraints explicitly; the remaining nine-factor product uses a `0.008` cutoff in product space. Detachment readiness continuously reduces this drive but cannot veto fusion merely because a cell is currently isolated. A successful fusion creates an immature persistent membrane junction at the actual contact pair; ordinary material remodeling, load, integrity, and detachment then decide whether that bridge survives long enough to transmit a program. Union roots are ordered by permanent cell ID; the component containing the oldest participating cell anchors physical ownership independent of thread scheduling. The transition appends a fusion event with both birth IDs. Reframed cells retain their own programs and acquired state, permitting mixed-program tissue without rewriting cellular inheritance. This is a dimensionless fusion and parasexual recombination rule, not a calibrated biological mating, histocompatibility, or symbiosis model.

For same-owner fragmentation, the largest membrane-connected root retains identity. Every other nonempty root claims a component handle immediately and receives a monotonic component birth ID plus one increment of component-descent depth. Existing cells retain permanent cell identity, acquired physiological and regulatory state, membrane vertices, and generation-valid inherited-program references. Fragments are never culled for observer criteria or aggregate biological measurements. A physical split alone is not called regenerative reproduction: a descendant flag is added only after one of its cells completes division. A scheduled impact marks a challenge only when at least one actual descendant cell is struck, clears prior homeostasis evidence, and starts a component-local recovery window; the second historical flag is restored only after that window closes with a multicellular state having combined ATP-reserve and ongoing-flux support, adequate integrity, and bounded stress. This avoids requiring one absolute ATP store when current substrate flux already closes cellular demand. These flags are read-only evidence outputs and never change viability. Individual cells persist or die through their local ATP balance, membrane damage, repair, stress, apoptosis, and contact mechanics; a component handle is recycled only after its last cell is gone.

## Permanent Genealogy

Component slots are reusable storage, not biological identity. Every founder and separated component atomically acquires a monotonic `birthID`; separated components store `parentBirthID`. Component birth, component death, physical fusion, cell division, program mutation, and crossbreeding append sequence-numbered 80-byte records to a 4,096-entry private GPU ring. Fusion records use `birthID` for the persistent component and `parentBirthID` for the incorporated contributor. Crossbreeding uses paired division and donor records so both exact parent programs survive slot reuse. The CPU reconstructs a component ancestry DAG rather than forcing fusion into a tree. Component-descent depth and program-replication generation remain distinct measurements.

Observation commands copy the event ring and its monotonic write counter into a triple-buffered shared ring. Swift reconstructs the parent-ID graph and computes path distance to the lowest common predecessor plus normalized morphology distance. A clade is reported only after combined genealogical, topology, and morphology separation persists for at least 1,200 steps. This observer analysis does not feed simulation state and is not called a species count because crossbreeding exists without a population-level reproductive-isolation criterion.

## Physical Scale

The finite backing textures can expand while the observed world remains continuous. `worldScale` records how much physical territory a normalized backing texture represents. Physical magnification is:

```text
observationZoom = cameraZoom / worldScale
```

When the backing world doubles, old ecology is copied into the central half, agent positions and velocity are halved, and `cameraZoom` and `worldScale` double together. The one cell-local-to-world length unit is divided by `worldScale`; therefore membrane radii, tissue extents, contact distance, translation, and separated-component position preserve physical scale without rescaling stored cell geometry.

The follow camera consumes ring-buffered GPU observations and tracks a permanent birth ID while translating it to the currently occupied storage slot for rendering. Slot reuse cannot transfer the camera to an unrelated organism. Previous, next, and horizontal trackpad navigation replace only the tracked birth ID.

## Environment

Persistent geology defines localized nutrient beds, mineral deposits, toxic vents, and rock obstacles, with large empty regions between them. Deposit supply is modulated by deterministic local phase and frequency fields rather than constant regeneration. Exact fixed-point cellular claims can exceed local replenishment and therefore generate persistent depletion gradients. Catalyst-dependent mineralization transfers bounded detrital fractions back into both substrate reservoirs; toxin and low permeability suppress that recycling.

Rock produces a local outward cell force, velocity damping, stress, and dissipative ATP cost. It also lowers field-level permeability. The extracellular mechanical equation receives deterministic geological oscillators with continuously varying local frequency and amplitude. Cells compare that frequency with their inherited resonator frequency and bandwidth. Mismatch under finite drive adds stress and dissipation. Constructed armor, predatory lobes, sensors, and locomotor extensions consume ATP; armor also increases reduced tissue mass/drag and lowers uptake. Contact attack and defense use the actual contacted arc and its directional construction, not a genome value alone.

These equations create resource-time, recycling, barrier, mechanical-spectrum, offense, and defense constraints without assigning a species or niche identifier. The corresponding observer channels are substrate forcing, detritus density, barrier fraction, environmental mechanical drive, per-cell barrier load, environmental frequency, frequency match, armor construction, and predatory construction.

## Long-Duration Experiments And Invariants

`NumiAutomata experiment` uses `EvolutionRenderer.encodeSimulationStep` without drawable acquisition or render encoding. One UInt32 seed enters initial geology and spinor phase; all later stochastic transitions derive from seeded state, integer simulation step, permanent identity, and local coordinates. A configurable number of biological steps is encoded per command buffer. Quantum evolution defaults to one update per three biological updates, matching the interactive default. Epoch generation and perturbation cadence are preserved.

Interactive execution admits at most three unfinished command buffers without blocking the main thread. Scheduled, GPU-completed, and scientifically committed counters are distinct; a failed or superseded callback cannot advance the committed counter. Every completion status and Metal error is inspected with encoder diagnostics enabled. A five-second completion watchdog stops new admission when submitted work ceases to complete. The application continues timed simulation submission while its window is covered or minimized and returns rendering to the `MTKView` cadence after activation.

Two alternating in-memory checkpoint banks capture the complete causal texture and buffer set every 1,200 committed steps together with observer-delivery counters and CPU evaluator state. Recovery invalidates callbacks from the failed submission epoch, recreates the command queue, restores the newest complete bank into both ping-pong states, removes observations newer than the checkpoint, and resumes the same world. The maximum rollback is 1,199 committed steps. Checkpoints deliberately do not survive application termination.

The terminal report keeps mechanochemical autonomy and collective-level individuality independent, and decomposes Darwinian evidence into physical descent, transmitted heritable variation, and statistically differential transmission. `darwinianEvolution` is supported only when all three terms are supported. Conditional self-predictive information uses empirical autocorrelation windows, moving-block bootstrap intervals, and block-shuffled nulls. `NumiAutomata causal-experiment` performs replicated paired interventions. SplitMix64 derives at least four distinct replicate seeds from one master seed. For each derived seed, control and treatment execute gain `1` through the configured intervention boundary. Control retains gain `1`; treatment changes to `0`. `NumiAutomata regeneration-experiment` performs the stricter paired wound qualification. Both same-seed branches disable ambient scheduled impacts. At the last baseline step, a one-thread GPU selection chooses the smallest permanent birth ID among living multicellular descendants that have completed post-separation cell division and stores one real living anchor cell. ## GPU Frame

Each active frame performs:

1. `reactWorld` samples the spinor and previous mechanical field, settles the previous cellular substrate claims, detrital returns, ligand secretion, matrix deposition, and wound/remodeling output, republishes fixed-point resource reservoirs, and evolves catalyst, stored energy, membranes, inherited fields, substrate ecology, and the extracellular developmental field.
2. `nucleateAutogenicFounder` atomically promotes one viable local maximum into the first persistent organism.
3. `evolveAgents` integrates translation and rotation from the previous cellular force/torque aggregate and removes identity only after tissue loss.
4. `evolveOrganismCells` atomically claims local A/B/detritus, closes ATP/work/heat/detritus accounting, senses extracellular ligand/matrix/wound state, advances voltage, inherited Ca*/ERK* signaling, refractory dynamics, attractor-capable sparse graph state, phenotype, fate, traction, secretion, and matrix remodeling over 9,216 slots.
5. `evolveCellMembranes` advances 110,592 membrane vertices, including regulatory construction forces for predatory, armor, sensory, and locomotor structures, and records polygon geometry and local integrity.
